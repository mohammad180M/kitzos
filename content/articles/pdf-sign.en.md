The print-sign-scan ritual refuses to die: print the contract, sign it with a pen, photograph or scan it, and email back a slightly crooked, slightly gray copy of what used to be a crisp document. For most everyday agreements, all anyone actually needs is your signature on the page — and that takes under a minute in a browser.

## Signing step by step

1. Open the Sign PDF tool and drop your document in.
2. Draw your signature — mouse works, but a finger or stylus on a touchscreen feels most natural.
3. Drag the signature onto the page, position it precisely on the signature line, and resize to fit.
4. Save and download the signed document.

The result is clean: your original stays sharp (no scanner noise, no skewed pages), with your signature exactly where it belongs.

## Getting a natural-looking signature

**Sign big, then shrink.** Draw your signature large in the signing box — scaled down, it looks smooth and confident. Drawing tiny produces shaky lines.

**Use your phone for the drawing.** If you're on a laptop without a touchscreen, opening the tool on your phone turns your thumb into a perfectly serviceable pen.

**Retry freely.** Clearing and redrawing costs nothing; nobody nails a mouse signature on attempt one.

## What this is — and isn't

A drawn signature like this is an **electronic signature**: a visual mark of agreement, the digital equivalent of pen on paper, and widely accepted for everyday documents — offers, invoices, internal approvals, rental forms. It is different from a **cryptographic digital signature** (certificate-based identity verification), which some governmental and high-stakes legal processes require. When a counterparty specifies a particular signing platform or certificate, follow their requirement; for everything else, this is exactly what's needed.

## Common questions

**Is a drawn signature legally valid?** For most everyday business and personal documents, electronic signatures are broadly accepted. High-stakes or regulated processes may require certificate-based signing — check the counterparty's requirements.

**Can I sign multiple pages?** Yes — place your signature on as many pages as needed.

**Is my signature stored anywhere?** No. Close the tab and it's gone.
