Everyone has met the sideways PDF: a scanner fed the page in landscape, a phone photo came in rotated, and now every reader has to tilt their head. The trap most people fall into is rotating the page *in their viewer* — which fixes it on their screen only. The moment you email that file, it arrives sideways again.

## Viewer rotation vs. real rotation

PDF viewers have a rotate button that changes how the page is *displayed* — a temporary, local courtesy that's usually not saved into the file. Actually fixing the document means rewriting each page's rotation property inside the PDF itself, so it opens correctly everywhere, for everyone, forever. That's what a rotation tool does.

## Rotating step by step

1. Open the Rotate PDF tool and drop your file in.
2. You'll see thumbnails of every page — instantly spot which ones are wrong.
3. Rotate individual pages 90° at a time, or apply a rotation to all pages at once.
4. Save and download the corrected document.

The thumbnail preview is the quiet hero here: in a 30-page scan where only pages 7 and 19 came in sideways, you fix exactly those two without guessing.

## Practical tips

**Mixed orientations are normal in scans.** Duplex scanners often flip every second page 180° — the "rotate all even pages" pattern. Check thumbnails before assuming the whole document needs the same fix.

**Rotate before signing or watermarking.** Overlays are placed relative to the page; adding them first and rotating after can leave your signature sideways.

**Landscape tables are sometimes intentional.** A rotated page in an otherwise portrait report may be a wide table meant to be read that way — fix orientation only where reading is actually broken.

## Common questions

**Does rotating reduce quality?** No. Only an orientation property changes; page content is untouched, byte for byte.

**Can I rotate just one page in a large file?** Yes — each page rotates independently.

**Will the rotation stick in every viewer?** Yes. It's written into the file itself, so Adobe, browsers, phones, and print all honor it.
