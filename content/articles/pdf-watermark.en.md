A watermark is a document's way of speaking for itself after it leaves your hands. DRAFT tells reviewers not to quote the numbers yet. CONFIDENTIAL reminds recipients before they hit forward. A company name quietly claims every page of a proposal. Whatever the message, it needs to be on *every* page — which is precisely what a watermark tool automates.

## When watermarks earn their keep

**Version control.** A bold DRAFT across a circulating document prevents the classic disaster: someone acting on preliminary numbers as if they were final.

**Confidentiality signaling.** CONFIDENTIAL or INTERNAL USE ONLY on each page keeps the sensitivity visible even when someone prints page 6 alone. It's a deterrent and a reminder, not encryption — pair it with password protection when you need actual access control.

**Attribution.** Your name or brand across shared work — portfolios, reports, proposals — travels with every page, wherever the file ends up.

## Getting it right

**Transparency is the craft.** The classic look — large, diagonal, subtle — comes from low opacity: unmistakable at a glance, yet the text underneath reads perfectly. If the watermark fights the content, lower the opacity before shrinking the size.

**Watermark last.** Apply it after merging, organizing, and signing, so the final page set is what gets stamped.

**Check a dense page in the preview.** A setting that looks elegant over white space may collide with a full-page table — preview your busiest page before applying.

## The privacy angle

There's a quiet irony in uploading a document to a stranger's server in order to stamp it CONFIDENTIAL. On Kitzos, the watermark is rendered and embedded entirely in your browser — the file never leaves your device, before or after stamping.

## Common questions

**Can the watermark be removed later?** It becomes part of the page content — not casually removable, though no visual watermark is cryptographic protection. For enforcement, add password protection too.

**Does it affect text quality?** No — the underlying pages aren't re-rendered; the watermark is layered onto them.

**Different watermarks on different pages?** The tool applies one watermark across all pages; for mixed schemes, split, stamp separately, and merge.
