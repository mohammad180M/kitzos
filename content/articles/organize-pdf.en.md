Some PDFs arrive almost right: the content is all there, but page 9 belongs after page 3, two blank pages snuck in from the scanner, and the appendix somehow landed in the middle. You don't need to split, convert, or rebuild anything — you need a page organizer.

## What organizing covers

An organizer shows your entire document as a grid of page thumbnails and lets you manipulate pages directly: drag to reorder, click to delete. It's the visual, direct alternative to juggling split-and-merge operations for what is really a single edit.

## Organizing step by step

1. Open the Organize PDF tool and drop your file in.
2. Every page appears as a thumbnail in a grid.
3. Drag pages into their correct positions.
4. Delete pages you don't need — blanks, duplicates, wrong inserts.
5. Save and download the restructured document.

Because you're looking at actual page previews, there's no page-number arithmetic and no "wait, was it page 14 or 15?" — you see what you're moving.

## Where this beats split + merge

Fixing one misplaced page via splitting means: split into three ranges, reorder the pieces, merge them back — three tools, three downloads. The organizer does it in one drag. Save split/merge for their real jobs (producing separate files, combining separate files) and use the organizer for everything happening *inside* one document.

## Practical tips

**Scan cleanup is the killer use case.** Batch scans routinely produce blank backs of pages and the occasional double feed — sweep them all out in one pass.

**Building a custom reading order?** Assembling a proposal where the summary must come first even though it was written last takes seconds here.

**Deleted the wrong page?** Your original file on disk is untouched — the tool always produces a new document, so just start over from the original.

## The privacy angle

The whole grid — thumbnails included — is rendered locally in your browser. The document is never uploaded; page previews are generated on your device, not on a server. Restructure a confidential report with the same privacy as doing it on paper.

## Common questions

**Does reordering affect quality?** No. Pages are moved, not re-rendered.

**Can I duplicate a page?** The tool focuses on reordering and deletion; to repeat a page, merge the file with an extracted copy of that page.

**Is there a page limit?** No imposed limit — very large documents are bounded only by device memory.
